Frequently Asked Questions

Everything You Need to Know.

Answers about eligibility, teams, judging, and logistics for the AIXelerate Build Sprint. Can't find what you're looking for? Email us.

Eligibility & Teams

Who can participate in the AIXelerate Build Sprint?
The event is open to middle and high school students (roughly grades 6–12, ages 11–18). No prior coding or AI experience is required — beginners are welcome and encouraged.
Can I compete alone, or do I need a team?
Either works. You can register solo and we'll help you find teammates at kickoff, or register with a team of up to 4 people already formed.
Is there a cost to participate?
No. The AIXelerate Challenge and the Build Sprint are completely free to attend.
Do I need to know how to code?
Not at all. Students can contribute through research, design, product thinking, presentation, or community insight — not just implementation.

Event Day & Logistics

When and where is the event?
July 25, 2026, from 10 AM to 7 PM at DMM Events, 3479 NE John Olsen Ave, Hillsboro, OR 97124 (Portland Metro Area). It is an in-person event.
What should I bring?
A laptop and charger are the essentials. Bring any personal items you'd like — snacks, a water bottle, or reference materials.
Will food be provided?
Food is not provided by the event. We recommend bringing snacks or a meal, as there may be limited options nearby.
How do I get to the venue?
Transportation is self-arranged. See the venue section on our Hackathon page for a directions link.

Tracks, Judging & Prizes

What is the challenge theme?
Build an AI-powered solution to a real-world problem within one of six tracks: Education, Sports, Mental Health, Community Impact, Public Safety, or Animal Welfare.
How are projects judged?
Judges score projects out of 100 points across five criteria: Innovation & Creativity (25), Impact & Relevance (25), Technical Execution (25), Presentation & Demo (15), and Feasibility & Scalability (10).
What can I win?
Cash prizes for the top 3 teams ($200 / $150 / $100), plus a sponsor prize of $50 in Claude credits. See the full breakdown on the Hackathon page.

Getting Involved

How do I register?
Head to our Join Us page and fill out the Student Competitor form. Seats are limited, so we recommend registering early.
I'm not a student — can I still help?
Yes! We're always looking for mentors, judges, volunteers, and sponsors. Visit Join Us or our Sponsors page to get involved.
