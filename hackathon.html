Overview

A structured build day, ending with a live expo.

The AIXelerate Build Sprint brings together students, builders, and innovators for a one-day hackathon focused on creating impactful AI-powered solutions to real-world problems. Teams move from kickoff and team formation, through a focused build window, into a judging expo and science fair where judges and attendees can see every project in person.

No prior AI experience is required — beginners are encouraged to participate. Students can contribute through research, design, implementation, product thinking, presentation, or community insight.

Competition Tracks

Six Areas for Practical Projects.

Build an AI-powered solution to a real-world problem. Select one primary track — projects may touch more than one area, but each submission should name the audience and problem it is built around.

Track 01

Education

Empowering learners and teachers through better access, feedback, organization, tutoring support, and classroom workflows.

Track 02

Sports

Enhancing performance, athletics, coaching, training safety, game strategy, scheduling, and team coordination.

Track 03

Mental Health

Supporting mindfulness, wellness, accessibility, stress management, resource navigation, and healthy routines.

Track 04

Community Impact

Solving local neighborhood and environmental problems with tools for service, coordination, awareness, and action.

Track 05

Public Safety

Helping communities prepare for hazards, share reliable information, coordinate response, and support vulnerable residents.

Track 06

Animal Welfare

Improving shelter support, adoption discovery, care coordination, habitat reporting, and veterinary access.

Event Schedule

July 25th Event Flow.

Times are listed in Pacific Time. The schedule is subject to change based on venue availability and final participant count.

10:00 AM

Kickoff & Team Formation

Opening remarks, event overview, rules and judging criteria, challenge theme reveal, and team formation for solo participants.

10:30 AM

Hacking & Build Time

Teams build their projects with a focus on MVP development. Mentors circulate to provide technical assistance, debugging support, and feedback.

2:30 PM

Submission & Setup Buffer

Hacking concludes. Teams submit project links and materials, then set up laptops and demos for the expo.

3:00 PM

Judging Expo & Science Fair

Judges circulate the venue to evaluate projects. Teams demo and answer questions as attendees explore the showcase.

4:30 PM

Judge Deliberation / Keynote

Judges finalize scoring while attendees enjoy a sponsor presentation, keynote, or networking session.

5:15 PM

Finalist Pitches (Optional)

Top-performing teams present live demonstrations to the full audience.

5:45 PM

Awards & Closing Ceremony

Recognition of winners, prize distribution, group photo, and closing remarks.

6:15 PM

Networking & Wrap-Up

Time to connect with mentors, sponsors, and fellow builders before the venue closes at 7:00 PM.
