AIXelerate Challenge Flagship Hackathon

July 25th, 2026

A focused virtual hackathon where high school students form teams, build practical prototypes, receive mentor feedback, and present their work to reviewers.

Register for the Hackathon
Registrations close strictly on July 16th, 2026.
Overview

A structured build day with real feedback.

The AIXelerate Challenge Flagship Hackathon is designed for students who want a clear, supportive environment for creating useful projects. Teams move from idea alignment to prototyping, mentor review, presentation, and judging in one focused event day.

No advanced coding background is required. Students can contribute through research, design, implementation, product thinking, presentation, documentation, and community insight.

Competition Tracks

Six Areas for Practical Projects.

Select one primary track. Projects may touch more than one area, but each submission should name the audience and problem it is built around.

Track 01

Education

Empowering learners and teachers through better access, feedback, organization, tutoring support, and classroom workflows.

Track 02

Sports

Enhancing performance, athletics, coaching, training safety, game strategy, scheduling, and team coordination.

Track 03

Mental Health

Supporting mindfulness, wellness, accessibility, stress management, resource navigation, and healthy routines.

Track 04

Community Impact

Solving local neighborhood and environmental problems with tools for service, coordination, awareness, and action.

Track 05

Animal Welfare

Improving shelter support, adoption discovery, care coordination, habitat reporting, and veterinary access.

Track 06

Public Safety

Helping communities prepare for hazards, share reliable information, coordinate response, and support vulnerable residents.

Event Schedule

July 25th Event Flow.

All times are listed in Eastern Time. Final timing may be adjusted slightly after registration closes.

9:00 AM

Opening Ceremony

Welcome, event expectations, judging overview, communication channels, and team check-in.

9:30 AM

Track Briefing and Problem Framing

Participants review track goals, define user needs, and narrow project scope before building.

10:00 AM

Team Formation and Idea Alignment

Solo participants and teams confirm roles, choose a track, and write a concise project statement.

10:30 AM

Build Sprint Begins

Teams begin prototyping, research, interface planning, data collection, documentation, or workflow design.

12:30 PM

Mentor Office Hours

Mentors review scope, feasibility, accessibility, privacy, and presentation direction.

2:30 PM

Progress Checkpoint

Teams submit a brief status update with their track, problem, user, prototype plan, and blockers.

4:00 PM

Prototype Refinement

Teams improve the core demo, prepare screenshots or walkthroughs, and document design decisions.

6:00 PM

Presentation Workshop

Teams organize their pitch around the problem, audience, solution, limitations, and next steps.

7:30 PM

Submission Window

Teams submit project links, slides or demo notes, team roster, track selection, and ethics statement.

8:00 PM

Final Presentations and Judging

Teams present live or recorded walkthroughs, followed by reviewer questions and scoring.

10:00 PM

Closing Session

Organizers share next steps for feedback, certificates, showcase review, and post-event resources.

Judging

What Reviewers Look For.

Projects are judged on usefulness, clarity, responsible design, execution, and how well the team explains constraints and next steps.

  • Problem Fit30%
  • Execution25%
  • Responsible Design20%
  • Presentation15%
  • Team Process10%
